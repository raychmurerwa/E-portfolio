Information Risk Management

10 Credits
Compulsory

Unit 1 - IRM: Definitions, Standards and Approaches

LEARNING OUTCOMES
. Review a number of common definitions of Information Risk Management.

. Review some common Information Security Management Concepts.

. Discuss the Risk Management Process.

This is the introductory unit which introduced me to information risk management. Unit one came with a lecturecast that was rich in definitions and valuable detailed information.
Group contract

Unit 2 - Users, Assessment & the Risk Management Process

LEARNING OUTCOMES
. Review the Risk Management Process (RMP).

. Review the effects of different assessment types (Qualitative vs. Quantitative).

. Discuss potential changes to the RMP based on changes in user participation.

This unit continued with the risk management process. It has a discussion forum that I participated in and posted my initial post. The discussion was a lovely learning ground..

Unit 3 - An Introduction to Risk and the SDLC

LEARNING OUTCOMES
. Review the some of the various methodologies used to implement the SDLC – focussing on the difference between Agile and Waterfall methods.

. Discuss where risk affects each phase of the SDLC – and how some methodologies mitigate against it.

. Evaluate modern approaches – with a special focus on DevOps.

This week we are supposed to submit our group assignment. The commitment and effort is matchless. I have put in the hours to make sure we have addressed all requirwmwnts of the question. I have also responded to two of my peers initial posts.

Unit 4 - Alternative Perspective on Risk and SDLC

LEARNING OUTCOMES
. Review some studies around risk and its impact on the SDLC.

. Discuss the different risks that can affect each stage of the SDLC.

. Discuss possible mitigations for the most common risks.

Unit 4 has a lot going on with SDLC as the main learning outcome. We have a discussion forum on risk and the SDLC phases. I have contributed my initial post and also learning from other posts.

Unit 5 - Risk, Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

LEARNING OUTCOMES
. Review how and why to create business continuity (BC) and disaster recovery (DR) plans.

. Discuss the main determining factors in BC/DR plans – including Business Impact Assessments (BIA), Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs).

. Discuss emerging trends in Information Risk Management..

A number of terms are introduced in Unit 5. They are all on the learning outcomes for the unit. We are also having number of team meetings to meet the assignment of unit 6 deadline. The learning outcomes of this unit do contribute a lot to our assignment as well.

Unit 6 - The Future of Information Risk Management

LEARNING OUTCOMES
. Discuss the trends that could contribute or determine the future of the information risk management (IRM) field.

. Review the NSA ‘hard problems’ and discuss what contribution IRM has to them.

. Create a wiki that contains answers to key questions about the future of IRM.

We have reached the final week and the pressure of two modules has been intense. The assignment is ready for submission with every team member putting their equal share of work. .

End of Module Reflection

Finally, we have come to the end of the Information Risk Management module with the University of Essex Online. These 6 weeks have been full of intense pressure but the knowledge gained is beyond compare. Starting this module in unit 1, I thought that I had a background to risk and it was going to be an easy road. However, as I watched the lecture cast and the readings, I saw that what I knew was very minimal. The lecture cast had a lot of definitions, risk management processes and identification. This progressed on well with the discussion forum on the Spears and Barki (2010) unit 2 reading. This reading was so rich and full of very useful information for our discussion forums as well as unit 3 and 6 assignments. The discussion forum was also a learning platform where the tutors took their time to respond to our initial posts and add more points to what we would have put down. Going through all the posts would provide a lot of information and knowledge. What I loved about this module was the team work due to the groups we were working with. As we got to unit three, we were supposed to submit our assignment that we had been working on from unit 1. We worked together well and I will not take anything from my team. By unit 3, my understanding of risk management had drastically changed and I was happy to learn more about SDLC which I was also learning of in the Secure Software Development module. This was a lovely component which made me understand risk in all the phases of the lifecycle. This was carried over to unit 4 where I got more information from the readings and the discussion forum. According to Alexandra Altvater (N.D) SDLC SDLC provides a well-structured flow of phases that help an organization to quickly produce high-quality software which is well-tested and ready for production use. I really loved this concept Unit 5 was also one of the best weeks with a lot of terms introduced and well explained in the lecture cast. The importance of a disaster recovery plan cannot be over emphasized. This is something that we also had to create in unit 6 assignment. Unit 6 also came with the NSA 5 hard problems is something to continuously take note of. Each of the 5HP sits squarely within the principles of risk, although the concept of risk itself, along with uncertainty, is discussed only implicitly in the 5HP (Scala et al, 2019). I found myself asking a lot of questions, for example is this really the future of IRM? As a group we discussed and agreed that a lot needs to be incorporated in cybersecurity risk with regards to uncertainty rather than a cost benefit approach or consequence analysis. Overall, I enjoyed the module. Looking back from Unit 1 up to unit 6, my knowledge and understanding of IRM has really changed and I am in a better position to make sound decisions that make use of the risk matrix, scoring risk and the cost benefit analysis.
