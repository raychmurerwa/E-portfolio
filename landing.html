Unit 1 -Unit 12 minutes

MODULE REFLECTION

Finally, we have come to the end of the Network and Information Security Management module with the University of Essex Online. Throughout the 12 weeks, I have really enjoyed the module. I am really fascinated by the knowledge I have gained as well as the practical experience I also managed to gain which has enabled me to have a different appreciation of computers and the devices around me. Team work was also something I loved about the module. We were put into groups and I was a member of group 4. The way we related as a team in Unit one evolved over time and by the time we got to unit 12 we were like family. Interpersonal and teamwork skills were acquired that I really appreciated. Looking back from Unit 1 where we started on the basic principles of Network Security, I was a novice in the subject. It welcomed me and gave me meaning of the module that I was embarking on. The medical mannequin case study which was also part of unit 1, was very educative and mind opening. This case study formed 3 important tasks of unit 1, 2 and 3. During these 3 weeks of discussion, I was amazed by how much I can learn from my peers as well as feedback from my tutor. I would like to acknowledge and appreciate the time my tutor put into responding to my posts and those of my peers. His feedback enabled me to gain the critical thinking and analysis skills as well as communication and literacy skills as I would research more so as to answer his questions on his feedback. Over the first 3 weeks, my understanding grew from basic to intermediate level. The learning outcomes of the 3 units were relatively structural. I could identify vulnerabilities within softwares and modern electronic devices. These include missing authentication and authorisation, SQL injections and missing encryption. Stride and Dread was a tool that I learnt to use to evaluate the threats and vulnerabilities. By the end of unit 3, I had managed to create my own website on AWS educate account. We also had a group website which we were meant to share with group 1.

However, we faced challenges with AWS and these persisted for a further three weeks. I was disappointed as I was enjoying the practical hands on experience I was getting. I wonder how it would have helped to get another educational account that functions just like AWS. We however managed to proceed without much drawbacks on all other aspects of the course. The following three weeks were the best weeks of the module. Week 4 introduced me to what I had been longing to learn. It was a more practical approach to the module as I learnt what penetration testing is and I had to scan websites using various tools like Ping, Dig, MTR, DNS,Nmap and Whois. It was interesting to learn that the different tools scan different targets. For example, Nmap can show you the geographic location of the target whilst Dig can evaluate DNS name servers. By week 6, we submitted our proposal as group 4. All team members put in their share of ideas and commitment. The team meetings were another thing I looked forward to especially during these three weeks. They were so informative and educative. We could share ideas, knowledge, help and experience. By week 7, I was no longer a novice. I was proud of myself and how I could scan and perform tests on so many devices even at work.

In unit 7, a new tool was introduced namely Kali Linux. It’s such an interesting tool and a tool worth using in the industry. My question was why did we wait until unit 7 to learn of this tool. Personally, I think probably I would have abused it since the skills and knowledge were still little. GDPR case studies and issues came in unit eight up to ten. GDPR is a regulating body in the EU which regulates against data breaches. A personal data breach is a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data (ICO. N.D) This is one of the most important aspect in Cyber security. These three weeks had a mixture of practical and literature involved. The case studies raised a lot of issues on directives and articles of the GDPR being violated in everyday life. Unit 11, we submitted our final assignment as a group. This assignment was in relation to the design proposal in Unit 6. We managed accomplish all the tests and obligations we had in Unit 6 and even had more tools in unit 11. The final assignment was more detailed with findings, conclusions and recommendations clearly distinguished. We shared tasks and I did the conclusion part of the assignment. We also had a debate in week 11. I loved my topic for debate of how the future of the internet relies on the adoption of IPv6 since I had really enjoyed the topic in unit 3. IPv4 is running out of IP addresses, thus IPv6 should really be adopted with the rollout of the associated security measures to curb the problem. IPv6 has close to 1028 times larger IP addresses that IPv4.

Finally, we came to unit 12 and this is the end of the module. Writing this reflection, I am reminded of the critical reflection skill that I am acquiring through this reflective piece. What an interesting journey. Apart from the educative skills that I gained, I also gained personal skills like time management. Being a full time employee and student is not easy but I managed to complete all the module tasks. Overall, I enjoyed the module. Personally, I have become more aware of my surroundings and interestingly, I have become more cautious of the applications I use, what data they collect from me, what questions they ask before I submit my data and how secure is the application I am using. The seminars, lecture casts and the e-portfolio component also contributed a lot to my personal growth and development thus giving me the much needed IT and digital skills. The link to my e-portfolio is above and all the artefacts relating to this reflection are in the e-portfolio. I am very grateful for the module and I can use it in my future endeavours. This module is indeed a stepping stone to my qualification and professional career.
